Howard Aiken

Forerunner of the modern electronic digital computer

Howard Aiken standing in front of the Harvard Mark I

Timeline of Howard Aiken's life:

  • 1900 - Born in Hoboken, NJ
  • 1923 - Got a BS degree in electrical engineering
  • 1937- Proposed the idea for a calculating machine
  • 1939 - Awarded his Ph.D. from Harvard University in Physics
  • 1943 - Completed construction of the ASCC
  • 1944 - Renamed ASCC computer to Harvard Mark I and given to Harvard University
  • 1946 - Aiken and Grace Hopper published three joint papers with the title The Automatic Sequence Controlloed Calculator
  • 1947 - Aiken complete the Harvard Mark II, a completely electronic computer
  • 1952 - Continued to work at Harvard on iterations of machines, such as Mark III and the Mark IV
  • 1961 - Retired from Harvard University and was appointed as Distinguished Professor of Information at University of Miami
  • 1961 - Started up Howard Aiken Industries Incorporated, a New York consulting firm
  • 1964 - Aiken received the Harry M. Goode Memorial Award, a medal, and $2,000 awarded by the Computer Society
  • 1973 - Dies at the at the age of 73
